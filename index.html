What's CHIP-8?

CHIP-8 was a simple, interpreted programming language developed in the 1970s for early microcomputers like the COSMAC VIP. It's notable for its role in the history of video game development and emulation. CHIP-8 programs are typically small and consist of a series of instructions that manipulate registers, memory, and a basic display. While CHIP-8 itself is quite limited, its simplicity makes it an excellent platform for learning about computer architecture, emulation, and game programming. Many emulators exist for CHIP-8, allowing enthusiasts to run classic CHIP-8 programs on modern computers.
